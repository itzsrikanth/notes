## Philosophical Aspirations

### On Technology
- Technology should **empower, not surveil**. If a tool requires sending personal data to a corporation, it's broken by design
- **Open source is the only trustworthy model** for AI — if the code isn't auditable, the privacy promises are meaningless
- **Edge AI is the future** — not because cloud is bad, but because privacy, latency, and cost all favor on-device

### On Career
- **Stay on the edge of the wave** — always be learning the next thing, not defending the last thing
- **Direction > raw effort** — the lesson from my scattered early learning. Focus curiosity, don't just unleash it
- **Mentorship matters** — seek it actively, and eventually give it back to others on the same path
- **Self-taught is valid** — every career pivot I've made was self-directed. Credentials are nice; shipped work is real

### On Impact
- Build things that **a non-technical person can use** — privacy shouldn't require a PhD or a Docker setup
- **One-person leverage through OSS** — I may be one engineer, but a well-built open-source project can impact millions
- **Don't just compete with Big Tech — make their surveillance model irrelevant** by building something better
